Week 1: Basic Setup & E Major Chord

Foundation Building - Your guitar journey begins!

üéØ This Week's Focus

Establish proper posture and learn your first chord. Quality over quantity - even 15 minutes of focused practice daily will build muscle memory.

üìÖ Daily Practice Routine (20-25 minutes)

‚è±Ô∏è Setup & Posture (5 minutes)

  • Guitar strap height: Guitar body at waist level when standing
  • Sitting position: Guitar rests on right leg (for right-handed), straight back
  • Pick grip: Hold between thumb and index finger tip, firm but not tense, small triangle of pick visible
  • Fretting hand: Thumb behind neck, not wrapped around

‚è±Ô∏è Fretting Hand Exercise (5 minutes)

One finger per fret exercise:

  • 6th string only: 1st finger 1st fret, 2nd finger 2nd fret, 3rd finger 3rd fret, 4th finger 4th fret
  • Press each finger down one at a time, keep previous fingers pressed
  • Focus on curved fingers and light thumb pressure
  • Play each note cleanly before adding the next finger

‚è±Ô∏è E Major Chord (10 minutes)

Finger placement:

  • 2nd finger ‚Üí 2nd fret 5th string (A string)
  • 3rd finger ‚Üí 2nd fret 4th string (D string)
  • 1st finger ‚Üí 1st fret 3rd string (G string)

Practice method: Strum all 6 strings, hold for 4 beats, release for 4 beats, repeat 20 times

‚è±Ô∏è Cool Down (5 minutes)

  • Gentle finger stretches
  • Shake out tension from both hands
  • Light wrist rotations

üîß Common Issues & Solutions

Problem: Muted or buzzing strings

  • Solution: Press fingertips just behind frets, not on them
  • Solution: Curve fingers more so they don't touch other strings
  • Solution: Check thumb position - should be behind neck
  • Solution: Trim fingernails on fretting hand

Problem: Fingers hurt

  • Normal: Fingertip soreness is expected for first 2-3 weeks
  • Solution: Take breaks every 10 minutes
  • Solution: Don't press harder than necessary
  • Building calluses takes time - be patient!

Problem: Can't reach all frets comfortably

  • Solution: Adjust guitar position - higher strap or different sitting position
  • Solution: Check neck angle - slight upward tilt helps
  • Solution: Hand size varies - technique adapts over time

Week 2: A Major Chord & First Changes

Foundation Building - Adding your second chord and first progression

üéØ This Week's Focus

Learn A major chord and begin chord changes. Chord changes are challenging at first - this is completely normal. Focus on accuracy over speed.

üìÖ Daily Practice Routine (25-30 minutes)

‚è±Ô∏è Warm-up & E Major Review (5 minutes)

  • Practice E major chord placement
  • Place fingers, strum, lift fingers, replace - repeat 10 times
  • Focus on muscle memory development

‚è±Ô∏è A Major Chord Learning (10 minutes)

Finger placement (all on 2nd fret):

  • 1st finger ‚Üí 2nd fret 4th string (D string)
  • 2nd finger ‚Üí 2nd fret 3rd string (G string)
  • 3rd finger ‚Üí 2nd fret 2nd string (B string)
  • Important: Don't hit 6th string (low E) when strumming

‚è±Ô∏è First Chord Changes (10 minutes)

E major to A major changes:

  • Start very slow: one change every 8 seconds
  • Sequence: E-E-E-E, A-A-A-A (4 strums each)
  • Count: "1-2-3-4-change" out loud
  • Goal: 5 clean changes in a row (speed doesn't matter)

‚è±Ô∏è Basic Strumming Introduction (5 minutes)

  • Simple downstrokes only on E major
  • Strum from 6th to 1st string (high to low)
  • Use relaxed wrist motion, not arm motion
  • Count "1-2-3-4" while strumming

üîß Common Issues & Solutions

Problem: Chord changes are very slow

  • Normal: This is expected! Muscle memory takes weeks to develop
  • Solution: Practice placing fingers without strumming first
  • Solution: Lift and replace same chord 10 times before changing
  • Solution: Visualize finger placement before moving

Problem: A major sounds muted

  • Solution: All three fingers on 2nd fret, different strings
  • Solution: Arch fingers higher - they may be touching other strings
  • Solution: Press firmly but don't overdo it
  • Solution: Check that you're not accidentally hitting 6th string

Problem: Fingers feel cramped on A major

  • Solution: Space fingers slightly apart on 2nd fret
  • Solution: Use fingertips, not finger pads
  • Solution: Keep thumb behind neck for better finger arch

Week 3: D Major & Power Chord Introduction

Foundation Building - Completing your first three-chord progression

üéØ This Week's Focus

Add D major to complete the E-A-D progression and introduce power chords - the foundation of rock music. Practice selective strumming with D major.

üìÖ Daily Practice Routine (30 minutes)

‚è±Ô∏è Warm-up (5 minutes)

  • E-A chord changes - aim for smoother transitions
  • Practice 10 changes, counting out loud
  • Focus on finger memory rather than speed

‚è±Ô∏è D Major Chord Learning (8 minutes)

Finger placement:

  • 1st finger ‚Üí 2nd fret 1st string (high E)
  • 2nd finger ‚Üí 2nd fret 3rd string (G)
  • 3rd finger ‚Üí 3rd fret 2nd string (B)
  • Critical: Only strum strings 4-1 (avoid 5th and 6th strings)

‚è±Ô∏è Three-Chord Progression (10 minutes)

E-A-D-A progression:

  • E major (4 beats) - A major (4 beats) - D major (4 beats) - A major (4 beats)
  • Count: "1-2-3-4" for each chord
  • Use metronome at 60 BPM if comfortable
  • Focus on clean changes, speed will come naturally

‚è±Ô∏è Power Chord Introduction (7 minutes)

A5 power chord:

  • 1st finger ‚Üí 5th fret 6th string
  • 3rd finger ‚Üí 7th fret 5th string
  • Only play these two strings
  • Mute other strings with palm edge on bridge

Week 4: Power Chord Expansion & Palm Muting

Foundation Building - Developing rock guitar fundamentals

üéØ This Week's Focus

Master power chords on both 5th and 6th strings, learn palm muting technique, and develop position changes. These skills are essential for rock, punk, and metal styles.

üìÖ Daily Practice Routine (35 minutes)

‚è±Ô∏è Warm-up (5 minutes)

  • Major chord review: E-A-D progression
  • Focus on clean, smooth changes
  • A5 power chord from last week

‚è±Ô∏è 5th String Root Power Chords (12 minutes)

Learn these power chords:

  • A5: 1st finger 5th fret 5th string, 3rd finger 7th fret 4th string
  • B5: 1st finger 7th fret 5th string, 3rd finger 9th fret 4th string
  • C5: 1st finger 8th fret 5th string, 3rd finger 10th fret 4th string
  • Practice moving between positions with metronome at 60 BPM

‚è±Ô∏è 6th String Root Power Chords (10 minutes)

Learn these power chords:

  • E5: Open 6th string + 2nd fret 5th string
  • F5: 1st fret 6th string + 3rd fret 5th string
  • G5: 3rd fret 6th string + 5th fret 5th string
  • Same finger pattern, different starting position

‚è±Ô∏è Palm Muting Technique (8 minutes)

Palm muting setup:

  • Rest palm edge lightly on bridge, near where strings meet bridge
  • Practice with E5: 4 muted hits, 4 open hits, repeat
  • Compare muted vs. open sound - muted should be "chunky"
  • Palm should barely touch strings, not press down

üîß Common Issues & Solutions

Problem: Power chords sound unclear or muddy

  • Solution: Only hit the two intended strings of the power chord
  • Solution: Press firmly enough for clear sound, but don't overpress
  • Solution: Check finger placement - should be just behind frets
  • Solution: Use pick edge, not flat pick face

Problem: Unwanted string noise

  • Solution: Improve palm muting technique
  • Solution: Rest picking hand palm lightly on bridge
  • Solution: Angle pick to avoid hitting unintended strings
  • Solution: Practice selective picking - aim carefully

Problem: Difficulty with finger stretches

  • Normal: Hand strength and flexibility develop over time
  • Solution: Start with lower fret positions (E5, F5, G5)
  • Solution: Warm up hands before practicing
  • Solution: Take breaks every 10 minutes

Problem: Palm muting too heavy or light

  • Solution: Very light contact - palm should barely touch strings
  • Solution: Position hand closer to bridge for lighter muting
  • Solution: Practice extreme muting, then back off to find sweet spot
  • Solution: Listen for "chunk" sound, not complete deadening

Week 5: Rhythm Foundation & Metronome Work

Foundation Building - Developing solid timing and rhythm skills

üéØ This Week's Focus

Master timing and rhythm - the foundation of all music. Playing in time is more important than playing fast. Every great guitarist has solid rhythm skills.

üìÖ Daily Practice Routine (35 minutes)

‚è±Ô∏è Warm-up (5 minutes)

  • Power chord review: E5-A5-G5 progression with palm muting
  • Focus on clean chord changes
  • Check palm muting technique

‚è±Ô∏è Metronome Introduction (10 minutes)

Getting started with metronome:

  • Set metronome to 60 BPM (beats per minute)
  • Listen to clicks for 30 seconds without playing
  • Practice hitting beat exactly with E5 power chord
  • Count "1-2-3-4" out loud while playing
  • One strum per click - listen for perfect alignment

‚è±Ô∏è Steady Rhythm Development (15 minutes)

4/4 downstroke pattern:

  • Pattern: Down-Down-Down-Down (all downstrokes)
  • Practice with E5, A5, G5 power chords
  • 8 beats per chord, focus on consistency over speed
  • Tempo progression: 60 BPM ‚Üí 70 BPM ‚Üí 80 BPM
  • Count out loud: "1-2-3-4, 1-2-3-4"

‚è±Ô∏è First Rhythm Pattern (5 minutes)

Down-rest-Down-Down pattern:

  • Count: "1-(silence)-3-4"
  • Rest means no strum, but keep counting
  • Apply to progression: E5-A5-G5-A5
  • Keep internal counting steady during rests

üîß Common Issues & Solutions

Problem: Can't stay in time with metronome

  • Solution: Start slower - try 50 BPM if 60 is too fast
  • Solution: Count out loud while playing
  • Solution: Practice just listening and clapping first
  • Solution: Focus on downbeats (1 and 3) initially
  • Solution: Use metronome with visual component (flashing light)

Problem: Rhythm feels stiff or mechanical

  • Normal: Feeling stiff is part of learning - groove comes later
  • Solution: Focus on accuracy first, feel develops naturally
  • Solution: Move your body - tap foot, nod head slightly
  • Solution: Listen to songs with strong beats

Problem: Losing count during rests

  • Solution: Count out loud, including rests: "1-two-3-4"
  • Solution: Keep internal pulse going during silence
  • Solution: Practice just counting without guitar first
  • Solution: Use foot tapping to maintain beat

Problem: Downstrokes feel weak or inconsistent

  • Solution: Use confident, strong downward motion
  • Solution: Pick should hit strings at slight angle, not flat
  • Solution: Follow through completely on each strum
  • Solution: Practice without chord - just strumming motion

üí° Essential Timing Concept

Why timing matters more than speed:

  • Music is communication - timing helps others follow you
  • Playing fast but out of time sounds sloppy
  • Playing simple but in perfect time sounds professional
  • Good timing makes everything sound more musical
  • You can always speed up later, but timing habits are hard to change

Week 8: 12-Bar Blues Foundation

Foundation Building - Learning the most important song form in popular music

üéØ This Week's Focus

Master the 12-bar blues - the foundation of blues, rock, and jazz. This progression appears in thousands of songs and is essential knowledge for any guitarist.

üìÖ Daily Practice Routine (40 minutes)

‚è±Ô∏è Warm-up (5 minutes)

  • Major and minor chord review
  • E-A-D and Em-Am progressions
  • Down-Up strumming patterns

‚è±Ô∏è 12-Bar Blues Structure Learning (15 minutes)

12-Bar Blues in A (using power chords):

  • Bars 1-4: A5-A5-A5-A5 (4 bars of A5)
  • Bars 5-6: D5-D5 (2 bars of D5)
  • Bars 7-8: A5-A5 (back to A5 for 2 bars)
  • Bars 9-10: E5-D5 (the "turnaround")
  • Bars 11-12: A5-E5 (resolution and back to start)

‚è±Ô∏è Blues Strumming Pattern (12 minutes)

Blues rhythm:

  • Simple Down-Down-Down-Down with palm muting
  • Focus: chord changes exactly on beat 1 of each bar
  • Count: "Bar 1-2-3-4, Bar 2-2-3-4, Bar 3-2-3-4" etc.
  • Start very slowly at 60 BPM

‚è±Ô∏è Blues Feel Introduction (8 minutes)

Creating authentic blues sound:

  • Add palm muting for grittier, more controlled sound
  • Slightly emphasize beats 1 and 3 (stronger strums)
  • This creates foundation for "shuffle" feel
  • Listen to how it sounds more bluesy than regular strumming

üéµ 12-Bar Blues Structure in A

12-Bar Blues Progression (Power Chords):

Bars 1-4 (A5):    A5 | A5 | A5 | A5 |
Bars 5-6 (D5):    D5 | D5 |
Bars 7-8 (A5):    A5 | A5 |
Bars 9-10:        E5 | D5 |
Bars 11-12:       A5 | E5 |

Each "|" represents one bar (4 beats)
Count: 1-2-3-4 for each chord

Power Chord Positions:
A5: 5th fret 5th string + 7th fret 4th string
D5: 5th fret 4th string + 7th fret 3rd string  
E5: 7th fret 5th string + 9th fret 4th string

Alternative (easier for beginners):
A5: Open 5th string + 2nd fret 4th string
D5: Open 4th string + 2nd fret 3rd string
E5: Open 6th string + 2nd fret 5th string
